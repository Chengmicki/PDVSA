PDVSA Legal Entity Structure

Full Legal Name

Petróleos de Venezuela, S.A. (PDVSA)

“S.A.” stands for Sociedad Anónima — similar to a joint-stock corporation or public limited company (PLC).

Legal Entity Type

  • Sociedad Anónima (S.A.) — State-Owned Corporation
  • Separate legal entity from the Venezuelan state (in theory)
  • 100% owned by the government of Venezuela
  • Operates under Venezuelan commercial law + special state mandates

Role & Legal Powers

  • Sole operator and regulator of Venezuela’s oil and gas reserves (by law)
  • Controls exploration, production, refining, transport, and exports
  • Can enter contracts, joint ventures, and production sharing agreements

Subsidiaries

  • PDVSA Petróleo, S.A. — upstream operations
  • PDVSA Gas, S.A. — gas production and distribution
  • CITGO Petroleum Corporation — U.S. refining & distribution (ownership disputed)

Legal Foundation

  • Ley Orgánica de Hidrocarburos — grants PDVSA oil & gas authority
  • Constitution of Venezuela — hydrocarbons as state property

Joint Ventures (Empresas Mixtas)

Foreign/private companies partner with PDVSA via mixed companies:

  • PDVSA holds ≥51% ownership (by law)
  • Licensed to explore and produce oil
Example:
PetroPiar, S.A. — PDVSA (70%) + Chevron (30%) — Orinoco Belt operations

International Subsidiaries & Assets

  • CITGO Petroleum Corporation (USA) — refining & distribution (via PDV Holding Inc.)
  • PDVSA Europa, S.A. (Spain) — European operations & investments
  • PDV Marina, S.A. — shipping (oil tankers)

Legal Instruments Used

  • Shareholder Agreements (Chevron, CNPC, Rosneft)
  • Joint Operating Agreements (JOA)
  • Service Contracts (foreign companies provide services)

Key International Legal Disputes

Case Summary Legal Impact
CITGO dispute (U.S.) Creditors sought to seize CITGO assets to recover arbitration awards vs Venezuela U.S. courts analyzed whether CITGO = alter ego of PDVSA/state
